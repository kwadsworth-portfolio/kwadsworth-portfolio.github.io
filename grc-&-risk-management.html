Project 3: Cyber GRC & Risk Management (Mini-ISMS)

Governance, risk and compliance initiative aligned to ISO 27001 principles, demonstrating how a lightweight ISMS and structured risk management can support better security decision-making for a small organisation.

← Back to Projects

1. Overview

This project focused on designing a lightweight Information Security Management System (ISMS) and risk management approach suitable for a small organisation. The aim was to demonstrate how governance, risk and control can be applied in a practical way without the overhead of a full enterprise-scale implementation.

2. Scope & Objectives

  • Establish a simple but robust cyber GRC framework aligned to ISO 27001 principles.
  • Create a risk register that links threats, likelihood, impact and ownership.
  • Define proportionate security controls mapped directly to identified risks.
  • Produce clear governance documentation (roles, reviews, responsibilities).

3. ISMS & Risk Design

I designed a mini-ISMS that could realistically be adopted by a small business. Key design elements included:

  • Asset identification and simple data classification based on business impact.
  • Risk register structure covering threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood and impact.
  • Risk scoring model to support prioritisation and treatment decisions.
  • Controls mapped to risks using ISO 27001 style control categories.

The emphasis was on traceability — showing exactly how each control helped reduce specific risks and support business objectives.

4. Governance, Policies & Procedures

Governance was treated as a core pillar rather than an afterthought. I:

  • Defined roles and responsibilities for risk ownership and approval.
  • Drafted core ISMS policies (access control, incident management, backup, acceptable use).
  • Outlined a simple review cadence for risks, controls and policy effectiveness.
  • Documented how exceptions and residual risks would be recorded and justified.

This allowed me to demonstrate how GRC practices support auditability and accountability.

5. Outcomes & Lessons Learned

The project delivered a practical GRC and risk management foundation that can be extended over time. Key outcomes included:

  • Clear linkage between business objectives, risks and mitigating controls.
  • Improved visibility of security risks for stakeholders and decision-makers.
  • A repeatable framework that can be scaled as the organisation grows.
  • Better understanding of how ISO 27001-style governance works in practice.

Future enhancements could include integration with tooling (e.g. GRC platforms, ticketing, SIEM) and more formalised metrics and reporting.
